Climate Risk Disclosure NLP Analysis

Text Mining SEC 10-K Filings for Climate Language

Languages: Python Category: Sustainability Analytics
Research Question How do climate risk disclosures in SEC 10-K filings vary across sectors, and are they increasing over time?
Methodology

Natural language processing pipeline applied to SEC EDGAR 10-K filings. Uses keyword dictionaries, TF-IDF vectorization, and topic modelling (LDA) to quantify and classify climate-related language in corporate disclosures.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: SEC EDGAR API (10-K filings with User-Agent header)
Sectors: Energy, Technology, Financials, Utilities, Consumer Staples
Period: 2019–2024 annual filings
Key finding: Energy sector averages ~39 climate keywords per filing vs. ~0 for tech
Key Methods
Keyword Dictionary
Custom climate risk lexicon: 'carbon', 'emissions', 'climate change', 'transition risk', 'physical risk', 'net zero'
TF-IDF Vectorization
Term frequency–inverse document frequency identifies words that are distinctive to specific firms/sectors
LDA Topic Modelling
Latent Dirichlet Allocation discovers latent 'topics' in disclosure text — e.g., physical risk vs. transition risk themes
Key Findings
1
Energy sector averages ~39 climate keywords per 10-K filing vs. near zero for tech companies
2
Climate disclosure volume increased 2.3× from 2019 to 2024 across all sectors, with a sharp jump post-2022 (SEC proposed rules)
3
LDA identifies distinct topic clusters: physical risk (flooding, wildfire), transition risk (regulation, carbon pricing), and opportunity (renewables, efficiency)
